## Liam Shaw

{{< figure src="/images/Liam-Shaw-headshot-grey-shirt.jpg" width="300" >}}

**Group leader**<br>
I'm an evolutionary biologist interested in bacteria, particularly the study of antimicrobial resistance (AMR). After a PhD at UCL, I spent several years at the University of Oxford. My first postdoc (2018-2020) was with [Sarah Walker](https://www.expmedndm.ox.ac.uk/team/ann-sarah-walker) and [Nicole Stoesser](https://www.ndm.ox.ac.uk/team/nicole-stoesser), after which I held a Sir Henry Wellcome Postdoctoral Fellowship (2021-2025) working with [Craig MacLean](https://www.biology.ox.ac.uk/people/craig-maclean). During that period I also spent time as a visiting researcher with [Tim Blower](https://www.blowerlab.com/) (Durham) and [Richard Neher](https://neherlab.org/) (Basel).

I moved to Bristol with an ERC Starting Grant in early 2026. I have interests across evolutionary microbiology – if you're interested in collaboration, get in touch!

Alongside research, I also write about science for a general audience. See my [personal website](https://liampshaw.github.io/) for more info.  

Contact: liam.shaw@bristol.ac.uk

## Matt Bird

{{< figure src="/images/MattBird.jpg" width="300" >}}

**Senior Research Associate**<br>
I’m a computational biologist with a broad interest in bacterial genomics, currently focused on AMR and wastewater metagenomics. After my MSc at the University of Bath, I worked as a Healthcare Scientist/Bioinformatician within the Gastrointestinal Bacterial Reference Unit (GBRU) for the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA). 
During this time I collaborated within [Paolo Ribeca](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=juUtwYAAAAAJ&hl=en), [Satheesh Nair](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=_j7g3RwAAAAJ&hl=en), [Anaïs Painset](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=qPbix6EAAAAJ&hl=en) & [David Greig](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=HYBH7JEAAAAJ&hl=en) on a variety of research ranging from AMR surveillance to national pipeline development.
